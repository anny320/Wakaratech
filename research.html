Academic Work

Peer-Reviewed Publications

Published research across ACM, IGI Global, and ICERI — co-authored with colleagues at iHub Research and partner institutions.

2015

Kids Hacker Camps in Kenya: Hardware Hacking Effectiveness in Skills Transfer

Wachira Ndaiga & Anne Salim

ACM · iHub Research

An evaluation of hardware hacking camps for children using Arduino and Raspberry Pi platforms. The study applied participant observation, video interviews, and iterative curriculum evaluation to measure the transfer of the 5Cs — Collaboration, Communication, Creativity, Critical Thinking, and Curiosity — among 100 young participants across multiple camp cohorts in Kenya.

2014

Mobile Governance: An Effective Tool to Fight Corruption in Kenya

Anne Salim & Nathan Wangusi

ACM · iHub Research

Examines the role of mobile platforms in anti-corruption governance in Kenya, analysing citizen-facing tools that enable reporting, accountability, and transparency in public service delivery. Situates mobile governance within the broader landscape of ICT4D and public sector reform.

2013

mGovernance: Use of Technology in Water Services Delivery

Hilda Moraa, Anne Salim & Albert Orwa

IGI Global · iHub Research

A foundational study on the application of mobile technology to improve water service delivery and accountability in Kenya. Explores how SMS-based and mobile-first government systems can close the gap between citizens and service providers in underserved communities.

2012

Technology in Solving Society's Water Problems in Kenya

Hilda Moraa, Anne Salim & Lillian Nduati

iHub Research, June 2012

Catalogued 20 water technology applications in Kenya — 10 active, 9 inactive. Developer interviews surfaced six failure factors: scalability gaps, lack of funding, insufficient support, partnership requirements, absence of government buy-in, and a competition-focused mindset. Provides actionable recommendations for sustaining civic tech in the water sector.

2012

Water Governance in Kenya: Ensuring Accessibility, Service Delivery and Citizen Participation

Hilda Moraa, Albert Otieno & Anne Salim

iHub Research, July 2012

Reviewed Kenya's water governance structure, legal framework (Water Act 2002), and citizen access challenges across Embakasi and Lang'ata constituencies. Examined the strategic potential of technology for transparency, participation, and service delivery — with findings that informed subsequent mGovernance policy discussions.

Read paper →
2012

Development of Local Educational Digital Content for Schools in Kenya Using Mobile Devices

Colaco, Makhanu & Anne Salim

ICERI 2012

Investigates the creation and deployment of locally-relevant digital educational content for Kenyan schools via mobile devices. Addresses the critical need for context-appropriate content as a prerequisite for meaningful digital learning outcomes in low-resource settings.

2013–

Growth of Mobile Education Platforms and Other EdTech

Anne Salim

Research Report

Analysis of mobile education platform growth trajectories, user adoption patterns, and enabling conditions for EdTech scaling across sub-Saharan Africa. Draws on longitudinal data and platform metrics to identify what separates pilots that scale from those that stall.

How We Research

M&E Methodology

Combining quantitative rigor with qualitative depth — because numbers without context miss the story.

👁️

Participant Observation

Embedded fieldwork in programme sites — workshops, classrooms, community settings — to capture what surveys miss.

🎥

Video Ethnography

Structured video interviews and session recordings to document learning behaviour and programme impact over time.

📋

Trainer & Facilitator Reports

Systematic data collection from programme staff, including observation checklists, curriculum logs, and incident notes.

🔁

Iterative Curriculum M&E

Embedding evaluation loops within programme cycles so findings feed directly back into design — not just into a final report.

📊

Platform Analytics

Usage data, growth metrics, and user behaviour analysis for digital tools and EdTech platforms.

🌍

Context-First Framing

Research designed from the ground up for African contexts — not imported frameworks retrofitted onto local data.
